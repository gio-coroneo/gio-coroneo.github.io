( Summary )An editorial project born from a dialogue with former Constitutional Court judge Marta Cartabia and the question she posed: what are the limits of justice? The book explores restorative justice, which works to rehabilitate the offender and to tend the wound left in the victim.

( Context )Editorial Design Course,

Work About Play CV^

( The Brief )The work grew out of an independent publishing course led by Anna Stainer built around the theme of freedom. The challenge was finding an angle worth examining — and from Cartabia's question it became justice and its boundaries.

The project starts from one question, posed in dialogue with former Constitutional
Court judge Marta Cartabia: what are the limits of justice? Rather than answer it,
the book sets out to find where those limits lie and what lies beyond.

( COVER )The book's weight and solidity—from its thick cover and iron case—mirror the weight of the subject. The cover isn't printed but carved away, laser-burned into the surface, evoking a wound: an absence, something missing.

( Structure )The book opens with a historical context of violence and of how justice has stepped in to address it. From there it moves into a wider second section that, starting precisely from the limits of retributive justice, turns towards another model.

( Restorative Justice )Restorative justice is presented as both discipline and legal instrument: a practice that puts victim and offender back into dialogue, working towards the rehabilitation of the one and the healing of the other.

Where the law ends, the work of repair begins.

( The Book )Typography, pacing and structure carry the reader from the weight of violence toward the possibility of dialogue, allowing the editorial form itself to embody and mirror the very argument that the book sets out to make.
