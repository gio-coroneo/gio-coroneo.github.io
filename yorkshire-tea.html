Yorkshire Tea
x World Cancer Day:
Let's have a proper squeeze.

( Summary )A campaign that places Yorkshire Tea inside World Cancer Day. With a knowing sense of humour it ties the tea bag — the brand's most recognisable object — to the testicles, turning testicular self-examination into a ritual as ordinary and British as making a cup of tea.

( Scope )( Brand Identity, Pack Design )

( Context )Brief and final review with KesselsKramer, London.

Work About Play CV^

( The Brief )KesselsKramer set a simple game: draw a brand and an annual observance at random, then pair the two with wit rather than force. My draw matched Yorkshire Tea with World Cancer Day, an odd couple I had to bring together convincingly.

Yorkshire Tea's identity rests on one daily ritual: the tea bag. The campaign recasts it as a stand-in for the testicles, making self-examination for testicular cancer feel as routine as the brew — early detection changes everything.

( Packaging )Two substantial changes reshape the Yorkshire Tea pack: the signature
red band turns the pale blue of World Cancer Day's testicular cancer campaign,
and the familiar sheep becomes a shepherd checking himself.

( Tea packet )For the tea bag we designed a new layout: on the back, five short steps built to work on two levels at once — the very same instructions for brewing the tea and, at the same time, those for a testicular self-examination.

Same ritual, different reason to check.

( branded PSA )It was designed an adv in a packaging-reveal format, where the tea ritual becomes a guide to cancer self-checks. A still-life on a white background with gloved hands walks through the 5 steps, ending on the final co-branded lockup.

( campaign )The packaging carries the idea all the way to the shelf. The familiar Yorkshire Tea box becomes the vehicle for the self-examination message, and the tea bag itself is reframed as a simple, everyday prompt to stop and check.
